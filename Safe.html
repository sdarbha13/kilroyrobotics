How to Stay Safe

The internet can be a very dangerous place. Without making informed decisions, cybercriminals, otherwise known as hackers, can make an attempt to steal or abuse one's information. For example, in May of 2017 an attack by the name of WannaCry was executed. It was a worldwide ransomware, which is a specific type of malware that demands money from users in exchange for their files. WannaCry was spread through emails using a tactic known as phishing, where the hacker pretends to be a professional business and persuades the user to reveal personal information. The ransomware spread like wildfire, infecting over 200,000 computers! Fortunately, the attack was shut down quickly and only received up to $100,000. WannaCry mainly targeted small businesses, but future malware may target normal individuals and users. It's always best to be prepared using these simple steps.

Attacks Occur Every 38 Seconds

Hackers are becoming more efficient and more dangerous everyday. Making smart choices on the internet can protect you from most of their attacks.

It Only Takes One Hack

One infected email or one simple password is all it takes to access personal information. Stay away from malicious emails and incorporate strong passwords to keep hackers out.

Realize the Situation

If by any chance you decide not to make an informed choice on the internet, understand the consequences of losing your personal information to a hacker.

Update the Software

Hackers usually attack software that is out-of-date. Updating your system, as well as installing security programs, can prevent the vulnerabilities of outdated programs from becoming a threat.

Stay Away From the Suspicious

If any website or WiFi seems skeptical, it always best to stay away. This makes it too easy for hackers to track your online activity and download malware onto your device.

Protect With Passwords

Almost all websites (this guide included) are protected with passwords. Create strong passwords and change them frequently. Two-factor authentication can be implemented to further protect your information.
